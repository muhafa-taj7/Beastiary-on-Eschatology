A Book of Wonders · Entry the Seventh

Al-Dābba

الدَّابَّةُ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ

The Beast of the Earth الدَّابَّةُ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ The Eighth Sign · The Speaking Beast · Marker of Believers and Disbelievers
✦

Physical Description

Al-Dābba is a real beast — not an allegory or metaphor in form — who will speak to the people. Some traditions suggest it resembles a camel in some aspect of its form, though no single authenticated hadith specifies its complete appearance. It will emerge from the earth — either from Mount Ṣafā, from beneath the Kaʿba, or from the desert — though no ṣaḥīḥ hadith has been authenticated about the precise location of its emergence. (ʿArīfī, The End of the World, pp. 407, 409.)

Lore & Significance

Al-Dābba is the eighth sign of the major eschatological events, appearing after the sun rises from the west. It is an apocalyptic creature that emerges to speak to mankind and to brand the faces of the believers and the disbelievers — thereby making a visible and permanent distinction between the two groups before the final reckoning. It will brand the Muslims and the non-Muslims alike, separating the faithful from the heedless at the time when repentance is no longer of avail. (ʿArīfī, The End of the World, p. 409.)

The Qurʾānic foundation for this creature is found in Sūrat al-Naml (27:82): "And when the Word is fulfilled against them, We shall bring forth for them a Beast from the Earth that will speak to them — for mankind did not have certainty in Our signs." Al-Dābba's emergence is thus linked directly to the failure of humanity to heed the signs of God while the time for repentance remained open.

Al-Dābba is not merely a creature of terror — it is a creature of distinction and judgment. Its primary function is not to destroy but to mark: to make visible what was always true, rendering the spiritual condition of each soul legible upon their very face. Its emergence signals the closing of a door — the end of the age of choice.
