Kitāb al-Asrār · Entry the First

On the Beast of the Earth

فِي ذِكْرِ الدَّابَّةِ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ

Al-Dābba الدَّابَّةُ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ "The Beast from the Earth" · One of the Ten Major Signs of the Hour
✦

Essential Attributes

Classification Portent of the Final Hour (أَشْرَاطُ السَّاعَة)
Qurʾānic Locus Sūrat al-Naml, 27:82
Nature Terrestrial; speaking and discerning
Function To mark believers with light and disbelievers with darkness; to announce the closing of repentance
Arabic Name الدَّابَّةُ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ

Scriptural Origins

وَإِذَا وَقَعَ الْقَوْلُ عَلَيْهِمْ أَخْرَجْنَا لَهُمْ دَابَّةً مِّنَ الْأَرْضِ تُكَلِّمُهُمْ أَنَّ النَّاسَ كَانُوا بِآيَاتِنَا لَا يُوقِنُونَ

"And when the Word is fulfilled against them, We shall bring forth for them a Beast from the Earth that will speak to them — for mankind did not have certainty in Our signs."

— Sūrat al-Naml (The Ant), 27:82

The tradition of Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim records, on the authority of Abū Hurayra (may God be pleased with him), that the Messenger of God ﷺ enumerated the ten great signs preceding the Hour, among which he named الدَّابَّة — the Beast — alongside the rising of the sun from the West, the descent of Jesus son of Mary, the emergence of the Dajjāl, and the release of Gog and Magog.

"Among the signs [of the Hour] are: the smoke, the Dajjāl, the Beast, the rising of the sun from the West, the descent of Jesus son of Mary, Gog and Magog, and three land-collapses…"

— Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Kitāb al-Fitan, no. 2901
❧

Physical Description

Al-Qurṭubī holds that its emergence from the earth of Makkah is the most well-attested opinion, citing seven narrations to this effect.

The scholars have disagreed greatly concerning the form and nature of the Dābba, for the authenticated narrations describe its attributes in terms that strain the capacity of human imagination. What is agreed upon is that it shall be immense in stature — Ibn Kathīr reports traditions placing its height between sixty and three hundred cubits — and that it shall emerge from the sacred earth bearing two instruments of prophetic authority: the Staff of Moses (عَصَا مُوسَى), with which it shall illuminate the faces of believers, and the Seal-Ring of Solomon (خَاتَمُ سُلَيْمَان), with which it shall brand the foreheads of the disbelievers.

As for the location of its emergence, the preponderant scholarly opinion, as marshalled by al-Qurṭubī in his great commentary, holds that the Dābba shall emerge from the sacred enclosure of Makkah — specifically from the Masjid al-Ḥarām or its environs near Mount Ṣafā. A dissenting tradition places its emergence from the Tihāma valley, while others name the region of the Masjid al-Kūfa. God, exalted be He, knows best.

Opinions of the Scholars

  • Ibn ʿAbbās (d. 68 AH): Described the Dābba as having the head of a bull, the eyes of a swine, the ears of an elephant, the horns of a deer, the neck of an ostrich, the chest of a lion, the colour of a leopard, the haunches of a cat, the tail of a ram, and the feet of a camel — a composite of created beings, signalling God's power over all forms.
  • Ibn Kathīr (d. 774 AH): In al-Nihāya fī al-Fitan wa-al-Malāḥim, he emphasises that the Dābba's primary function is not destruction but distinction — to separate the believer from the unbeliever when repentance is no longer of avail.
  • Al-Suyūṭī (d. 911 AH): Collects twenty-two hadiths pertaining to the Dābba in his al-Durr al-Manthūr, favouring the interpretation that it will speak the Arabic tongue and recite Qurʾānic verses.

Thus concludes the first entry of the Bestiary of Signs.
