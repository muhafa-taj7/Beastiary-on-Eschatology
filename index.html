Kitāb al-Asrār

The Book of Secrets

كِتَابُ الْأَسْرَارِ

Concerning the Eschatological Creatures of Islamic Cosmology
A Bestiary of Signs and Portents Before the Hour

Compiled and arranged after the manner of the great wonder-books,
drawing upon the Holy Qurʾān, the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ,
and the authoritative commentaries of the scholars of Islam.

✦ ✦ ✦
❧

Know, O seeker of understanding, that among the greatest of the signs preceding the Day of Resurrection are those creatures and beings that the Almighty has prepared as witnesses against the heedless and as tokens of His boundless power. Their descriptions have been preserved in the sacred sources; herein they are gathered for the benefit of the student and the contemplation of the faithful.

✦

Index of Creatures & Wonders

  • I. The Dābba — Beast of the Earth الدَّابَّة Among the ten greatest signs of the Hour; the creature who shall speak to mankind and mark the faces of the believers and the disbelievers. fol. 3r
  • II. The Burāq — Celestial Steed of the Prophets الْبُرَاق The luminous beast ridden by the prophets; its stride reaches as far as the eye can see; the conveyance of the Night Journey. fol. 9v
  • III. The Jassāsa — The Spy of the Dajjāl الْجَسَّاسَة [Entry pending; manuscript lacuna] fol. 15r
  • IV. The Yaʾjūj wa-Maʾjūj — Gog and Magog يَأْجُوجُ وَمَأْجُوجُ [Entry pending; manuscript lacuna] fol. 22v
❧
A Note on Sources: The descriptions herein are drawn primarily from Sūrat al-Naml (27:82) and Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ (21:96) of the Holy Qurʾān; the collections of Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim and Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; and the eschatological commentaries of Ibn Kathīr, al-Suyūṭī, and al-Qurṭubī. Visual representations follow the pictorial traditions of the Wonders of Creation (ʿAjāʾib al-makhlūqāt) manuscripts of al-Qazwīnī (d. 682 AH / 1283 CE).
