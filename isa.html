A Book of Wonders · Entry the Fifth

Jesus / ʿĪsā ibn Maryam

عِيسَى ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ

ʿĪsā ibn Maryam — The Messiah عِيسَى ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ The Second Sign · Prophet of God · The Just Judge
✦

Physical Description

ʿĪsā ibn Maryam is of average height, with reddish-white skin and a broad chest. His hair is straight, appearing as though it is dripping with water even when it is not wet at all. He will come down at the white minaret in the east of Damascus, wearing two garments dyed with safflower and saffron, resting his hands on the wings of two angels. (ʿArīfī, The End of the World, p. 348.)

Lore & Significance

ʿĪsā is the second sign of the major events of the end of times. He was born of a virgin birth from Maryam (peace be upon her), spoke in the cradle, and God took him up before he was crucified. He descends in Damascus as a just judge. He will break the cross, kill the pigs, abolish the jizyah, and cause mutual hatred and envy to disappear — as will greed. (ʿArīfī, The End of the World, p. 337.)

He will pray behind the Mahdī, signifying the unity of the Muslim ummah and confirming that ʿĪsā himself is Muslim. He will then pursue and kill the Dajjāl: when the Dajjāl sees him, he will begin melting as salt melts in water. ʿĪsā will chase him and catch up with him at the gate of Ludd (in Palestine) and kill him with a spear. The trees, rocks, and mud will tell him where the followers of the Dajjāl are hiding — all except the Gharqad tree (box thorn), which is of their company. (ʿArīfī, The End of the World, pp. 309–310.)

ʿĪsā is a messianic figure — God can fix everything, and he represents purity. God can do things that we cannot comprehend, and He has ultimate control. The idea that ʿĪsā will destroy the cross is deeply significant: he will fundamentally destroy the central symbol of Christianity, and while Christians believe him to be the Messiah, he will himself reject the idea that he is the Son of God. Everything worships Allah — everything is under His control, except that which He commands to stand apart. The trees and rocks crying out against the enemies of truth is a sign that the entire created order is aligned with divine justice.
