A Book of Wonders

Al-Jassāsa - The Spy

الْجَسَّاسَة

Physical Description

Al-Jassāsa is a beast with a great deal of hair, to the extent that those who encounter it cannot distinguish its face from its back. It is described as a female monster of fabulous and fearsome aspect, associated with a remote island far out at sea. Those who meet it cannot determine its gender or form with certainty due to its immense amount of hair.

Lore & Significance

From the Ḥadīth of Fāṭima bint Qays · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim When the Messenger of Allah had finished his prayer, he sat on the Minbar and he was smiling... I have called you together because Tameem Al-Daari was a Christian and he came and swore allegiance and became Muslim, and told me something which agrees with what I was telling you about the Dajjaal (false Messiah).

He told me that he sailed in a ship with thirty men of Lakhm and Judhaam and they were tossed by the waves of the sea for a month. They came to an island at sunset. They sat in a small rowing boat and landed on that island. They were met by a beast with a great deal of hair and they could not distinguish his face from his back because he was so hairy. They said: "Woe to you, what are you?" It said: "I am Al-Jassaasah."

They said:"What is al-Jassasa?" And it said: "You go to this very person in the monastery, for he is eagerly waiting for you to know about you." So we came to you in hot haste, fearing that that might be the Devil. He (that chained person) said: "I am going to tell you about myself and I am Dajjal..."

Tamīm al-Dārī recounts his journey sailing in a ship with thirty men, tossed by the waves of the sea for a month. They came to an island at sunset and were met by a beast with a great deal of hair, and they could not distinguish its face from its back because it was so hairy. They said: "Woe to you — what are you?" It said: "I am al-Jassāsa." She then directed them toward a man chained in a monastery on the island, who was the Dajjāl, waiting for his appointed time of release. He begins asking about specific things, including the date palms of Baisan, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, and the lake of Tabariyya.

Al-Jassāsa has two primary roles: guardian and spy. She watches over the island, collects information about the world for the Dajjāl, and directs strangers toward him. However, Al-Jassāsa is a highly mysterious figure in Islamic eschatology. Knowledge about her is very minimal and her story has unusual narrative authority as it reaches us through the direct testimony of a companion of the Prophet ﷺ, which the Prophet ﷺ himself confirmed publicly on the mimbar.
